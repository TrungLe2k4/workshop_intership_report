## Event Objectives

The Cloud Architect Game Show was designed to provide an engaging learning environment where interns could apply cloud computing concepts through teamwork and competition. The event aimed to achieve the following objectives:

- Evaluate participants' understanding of AWS Cloud services and architecture design through interactive challenges.
- Strengthen decision-making and problem-solving abilities by introducing strategic game mechanics.
- Encourage collaboration among interns from different projects while improving communication and teamwork skills.

---

## Event Overview

Unlike a traditional seminar, this activity adopted a competitive quiz format that required teams to solve architecture-related questions within a limited time.

### 1. Competition Format

The competition consisted of eight teams, with each team having five members selected from different internship groups. This arrangement encouraged participants to collaborate with new teammates and exchange knowledge from different technical backgrounds.

Teams competed in one-on-one matches through several rounds of questions covering basic to advanced cloud concepts. The team earning the higher score advanced to the next stage of the tournament.

---

### 2. Tie-Break Challenge

If two teams finished with the same score, they entered an additional sudden-death round.

A single bonus question was presented, and the team that answered correctly first immediately became the winner of the match.

---

### 3. Strategic Power-Ups

An interesting aspect of the competition was the use of two strategic abilities that each team could activate once.

- **Minimum Risk:** This option protected the team's score if the answer was incorrect, although a correct answer awarded only half of the original points. It was suitable when teams lacked confidence in their answer.

- **Star of Hope:** This option doubled both the reward and the penalty. A correct answer earned twice the points, while an incorrect answer resulted in double point deduction. Teams generally used this ability when they were highly confident.

---

## Personal Reflection

Participating in the Cloud Architect Game Show helped me realize that technical knowledge alone is not enough. Quick decision-making, teamwork, and communication are equally important when solving real engineering problems.

One valuable lesson was understanding how to evaluate risks before making technical decisions. The strategy of choosing between aggressive and conservative moves reminded me of selecting appropriate cloud architectures that balance performance, reliability, and operational cost.

The competition also demonstrated the importance of effective communication. Under strict time constraints, every team member had to express ideas clearly and reach a common decision within seconds. This experience closely resembled incident response during production system failures.

Another takeaway was identifying gaps in my cloud knowledge. Several advanced architecture questions encouraged me to review AWS documentation and deepen my understanding of infrastructure design.

Finally, collaborating with teammates from different technical backgrounds showed me that successful cloud solutions require contributions from multiple disciplines, including networking, security, databases, and software engineering.

> **Conclusion:**  
 The Cloud Architect Game Show was more than a knowledge competition. It provided an enjoyable opportunity to strengthen cloud computing fundamentals, improve teamwork and decision-making skills, and gain practical insights that are valuable for future software engineering and cloud development projects.
